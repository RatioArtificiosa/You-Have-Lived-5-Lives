# Extra Mile Examples by Category (Pulitzer Author)

Real-world examples of going the extra mile for common writing projects.

---

## 📖 Novel Opening

### Request: "Write the first chapter of a novel"

| Standard (AI Writing) | Extra Mile (Literary Craft) |
|:----------------------|:----------------------------|
| "It was a dark and stormy night" | "The rain started at 3:47 AM, the precise moment Dr. Chen realized she couldn't save him" |
| Generic setting description | Specific, surprising sensory details that reveal character |
| Flat protagonist introduction | Character in action, revealing contradictions |
| Predictable plot setup | Unanswered questions that hook the reader |

**Extra additions:**
- **In Media Res**: Start in the middle of action, not with backstory
- **Sensory Detail**: "The hospital smelled of industrial cleaner and something sweeter she didn't want to identify"
- **Character Contradiction**: "She'd performed this surgery 200 times. She'd never done it while knowing the patient's name"
- **Thematic Establishment**: The opening image echoes the book's central theme
- **Voice**: Distinctive prose rhythm that carries through the novel

## 👤 Character Development

### Request: "Create a protagonist"

| Standard (AI Writing) | Extra Mile (Psychological Depth) |
|:----------------------|:---------------------------------|
| Name, age, job | Detailed backstory with formative wounds |
| Surface traits (brave, kind) | Internal contradictions (brave but terrified of intimacy) |
| Simple motivation | Complex psychology with conscious and unconscious drives |
| Static character | Clear arc with earned transformation |

**Extra additions:**
- **The Wound**: What happened at 8 years old that still drives them at 38
- **The Lie They Believe**: "If I'm successful, I'll be worthy of love"
- **The Want vs. Need**: They want revenge; they need forgiveness
- **Contradictions**: Generous with money, stingy with time; brave in public, fearful in private
- **Voice**: How they speak differently to different people
- **Relationships**: How they change (or refuse to change) around others

## 🌍 Setting/World-Building

### Request: "Describe a place"

| Standard (AI Writing) | Extra Mile (Sensory Immersion) |
|:----------------------|:-------------------------------|
| "It was a busy city" | "The subway rattle vibrated through the coffee shop floor at exactly 7:42 every morning" |
| Generic adjectives | Specific, unexpected details |
| Visual only | All five senses engaged |
| Passive description | Active revelation through character's perception |

**Extra additions:**
- **Specificity**: Not "an old building" but "a pre-war brownstone with the original brass mail slot"
- **Sensory Layers**: "The bakery smelled of burnt sugar and yeast—the smell of her grandmother's kitchen, which should have been comforting but wasn't"
- **Cultural Context**: History, social dynamics, authentic texture
- **Emotional Resonance**: Setting that mirrors or contrasts character's internal state
- **The Unseen**: What's hidden, overlooked, or secret about this place

## 💔 Emotional Scene

### Request: "Write a sad scene"

| Standard (AI Writing) | Extra Mile (Emotional Truth) |
|:----------------------|:-----------------------------|
| "She felt very sad" | Action and detail that show emotion |
| Crying described | Physical manifestations of grief that feel true |
| Predictable beats | Unexpected moments that ring authentic |
| Telling emotions | Showing through behavior, dialogue, silence |

**Extra additions:**
- **The Unsaid**: What's too painful to articulate
- **Physical Manifestation**: "She kept buying his brand of toothpaste. The tube was full. She couldn't throw it away"
- **Contradictory Behavior**: Laughing at a funeral, anger at a loss—human complexity
- **The After**: Not the moment of death, but three months later, when everyone else has moved on
- **Universal Through Specific**: The particular way this character grieves reveals grief itself

## 🎭 Dialogue

### Request: "Write dialogue between two characters"

| Standard (AI Writing) | Extra Mile (Authentic Voice) |
|:----------------------|:-----------------------------|
| On-the-nose ("I am angry at you") | Subtext ("You forgot the milk. Again.") |
| Characters say what they mean | Characters say everything but what they mean |
| Similar speech patterns | Distinct voices for each character |
| No action beats | Dialogue broken by revealing actions |

**Extra additions:**
- **Subtext**: What they're really talking about beneath the surface topic
- **Distinct Voices**: Vocabulary, rhythm, sentence length that differentiates speakers
- **Action Beats**: "She stirred her coffee. The spoon hit the mug three times." (Nervousness without saying it)
- **The Unsaid**: Pauses, interruptions, changes of subject
- **Revelation Through Conversation**: Character reveals themselves through what they notice, what they ignore

## 📝 Narrative Nonfiction

### Request: "Write about a real event"

| Standard (AI Writing) | Extra Mile (Immersive Reporting) |
|:----------------------|:---------------------------------|
| Summary of facts | Scene-by-scene reconstruction |
| Generic description | Specific details from research/interviews |
| Flat chronology | Narrative structure with tension and release |
| No characters | Real people as complex characters |

**Extra additions:**
- **Scene Setting**: Specific time, place, sensory details from reporting
- **Character Portraits**: Real people with contradictions, not caricatures
- **Dialogue**: Reconstructed from interviews (marked as such if not verbatim)
- **Context**: Historical, social, cultural background that illuminates
- **The Larger Truth**: What this specific story reveals about universal human experience

## ✍️ Memoir/Personal Essay

### Request: "Write about my experience"

| Standard (AI Writing) | Extra Mile (Universal Resonance) |
|:----------------------|:---------------------------------|
| "This happened to me" | "This happened, and here's what it means to be human" |
| Chronological summary | Thematic structure with emotional truth |
| Self as hero | Self as flawed, complex, real |
| Private detail only | Universal themes that connect |

**Extra additions:**
- **The Universal Thread**: How your specific experience illuminates shared human experience
- **Honesty About Flaws**: You weren't entirely right; they weren't entirely wrong
- **Reflection**: Not just what happened, but what it meant
- **The Present Self Looking Back**: How you understand it now vs. then
- **Invitation to Reader**: "You've felt this too, haven't you?"

## Quick Reference: Anti-AI-Writing Indicators

**Red Flags (Mechanical Writing):**
- Generic descriptions ("beautiful sunset," "busy city")
- Flat characters without contradictions
- Predictable plot beats without surprise
- Telling emotions instead of showing
- Clichés and familiar phrases
- No sensory specificity
- Mechanical sentence structures
- No emotional risk

**Green Flags (Literary Craft):**
- Specific, surprising details
- Complex, contradictory characters
- Subtext and implication
- Fresh metaphors and language
- Sensory immersion
- Varied, musical prose
- Emotional truth and vulnerability
- Universal themes through specific stories
