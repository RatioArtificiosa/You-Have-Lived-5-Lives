## Research Phase (Non-Negotiable)

### 1. Counterparty Deep Intelligence

**Company/Organization Research:**
- [ ] Recent financial performance (quarterly reports, earnings calls)
- [ ] Current strategic priorities (annual reports, CEO statements)
- [ ] Recent M&A activity or major deals
- [ ] Competitive pressures and market position
- [ ] Key executives and their backgrounds
- [ ] Board composition and investor base
- [ ] Legal issues or regulatory pressures
- [ ] Recent layoffs, hiring freezes, or expansion

**Individual Profiling (Decision Makers):**
- [ ] LinkedIn history (career trajectory, promotions, job changes)
- [ ] Published articles, interviews, podcast appearances
- [ ] Social media presence (Twitter, professional networks)
- [ ] Speaking engagements and conference appearances
- [ ] Educational background and affiliations
- [ ] Previous companies and roles
- [ ] Mutual connections for intelligence gathering
- [ ] Personality indicators from communication style

**Negotiation-Specific Research:**
- [ ] Their typical deal structures (from public filings, news)
- [ ] Past negotiation patterns (Glassdoor, industry gossip)
- [ ] Who they typically negotiate against
- [ ] Their win/loss record in similar deals
- [ ] Time pressure factors (quarterly earnings, fiscal year, project deadlines)

### 2. Market Intelligence

**Industry Analysis:**
- [ ] Current market conditions (growing, shrinking, disrupted)
- [ ] Recent comparable deals (precedent transactions)
- [ ] Standard terms and market norms
- [ ] Regulatory environment (changing, stable, uncertain)
- [ ] Supply/demand dynamics (who has leverage?)

**Competitive Landscape:**
- [ ] Their competitors' recent deals
- [ ] Your alternatives (other buyers, other sellers)
- [ ] Their alternatives (other suppliers, other candidates)
- [ ] Switching costs and barriers
- [ ] Market pricing benchmarks

### 3. Psychological Intelligence

**OCEAN Profile Assessment:**
- [ ] **Openness**: Do they embrace innovation or prefer tradition? (Look for: startup background vs. big corp, education diversity, creative hobbies)
- [ ] **Conscientiousness**: Are they detailed and organized or flexible and spontaneous? (Look for: career trajectory, achievements, published work)
- [ ] **Extraversion**: Do they seek social interaction or prefer depth? (Look for: networking activity, communication style, role type)
- [ ] **Agreeableness**: Are they harmony-focused or competitive? (Look for: conflict handling, negotiation style, leadership approach)
- [ ] **Neuroticism**: Are they anxious and risk-averse or confident and bold? (Look for: career risks taken, public statements, decision speed)

**MBTI Type Indicators:**
- Communication preferences (email vs. call vs. in-person)
- Decision-making style (data-driven vs. intuitive)
- Response to pressure (fight vs. flight vs. freeze)
- Social cues and energy management

**Cultural Factors:**
- National culture (high-context vs. low-context)
- Industry culture (tech vs. finance vs. healthcare)
- Company culture (aggressive vs. collaborative)
- Generational factors (boomer vs. millennial vs. gen-z styles)

## Tactical Intelligence Gathering

### The Pre-Negotiation Intelligence Phase

**Week 1: Public Information Sweep**
- Google News alerts for company and key individuals
- SEC filings and financial reports
- LinkedIn company page activity
- Glassdoor reviews (filter for interviews, compensation discussions)
- Patent filings and product announcements
- Conference proceedings and webinar recordings

**Week 2: Network Intelligence**
- Contact mutual connections for insights
- Attend industry events where they speak
- Engage with their content (thoughtful comments, shares)
- Request informational interviews with former employees
- Join industry forums and Slack communities

**Week 3: Direct Intelligence**
- Initial conversations (information gathering disguised as casual)
- Email exchanges (assess response time, communication style)
- Phone/video calls (voice tone, body language, environment)
- Trial negotiation on small issues (test their style)

## Advanced Negotiation Intelligence: The Shadow Game

### Information Asymmetry Creation

**What They Shouldn't Know:**
- Your true timeline urgency
- Your actual reservation point
- Your competing alternatives in detail
- Your internal constraints and pressures
- Your decision-making process

**What You Should Discover:**
- Their true decision-makers
- Their internal budget and constraints
- Their competing alternatives
- Their timeline and urgency
- Their reservation point indicators

### Counter-Intelligence

**Protect Your Information:**
- [ ] Don't reveal timeline unless necessary (and if so, lie about having more time)
- [ ] Never disclose true bottom line
- [ ] Create artificial competition if none exists
- [ ] Use "I need to check with my team/partner/board" as delay tactic
- [ ] Be vague about alternatives while emphasizing you have them

**Detect Their Intelligence Gathering:**
- [ ] Note unusual questions about internal matters
- [ ] Watch for fishing expeditions about constraints
- [ ] Recognize when they're stalling to gather more info
- [ ] Identify when they're using social engineering

## Scenario Planning & War Gaming

### The 5 Scenario Framework

Before any high-stakes negotiation, map these scenarios:

**Scenario 1: Best Case**
- They accept your opening position
- No significant concessions needed
- Deal exceeds target
- Relationship strengthened

**Scenario 2: Target Case**
- You achieve your goal with reasonable concessions
- Trade-offs are balanced
- Deal is profitable/satisfactory
- Relationship maintained

**Scenario 3: Walk-Away Case**
- They refuse to meet reservation point
- Deal would be worse than BATNA
- You walk away professionally
- Relationship preserved for future

**Scenario 4: Worst Case (Within Deal)**
- You make major concessions
- Deal barely meets minimum criteria
- Relationship strained
- You learn for next time

**Scenario 5: Dirty Tricks Case**
- They use deception or manipulation
- Unexpected stakeholders emerge
- Terms change at last minute
- You're prepared with counter-measures

### War Gaming Exercise

**Step 1:** Write out their likely opening position
**Step 2:** Write out their likely tactics and moves
**Step 3:** Script your responses to each
**Step 4:** Identify where negotiations might derail
**Step 5:** Prepare your recovery moves
**Step 6:** Practice with a colleague playing their role

## The 48 Laws in Action - Deep Application

### Law 3: Conceal Your Intentions
**Application:** Never reveal your true priorities. If salary matters most, emphasize equity or benefits. Let them believe you care about different elements.

### Law 4: Always Say Less Than Necessary
**Application:** In negotiations, silence creates pressure. After making an offer, shut up. The first to speak loses.

### Law 6: Court Attention at All Cost
**Application:** Control the agenda. Set the meeting. Choose the venue. Frame the conversation.

### Law 9: Win Through Your Actions, Never Through Argument
**Application:** Demonstrate value before discussing price. Show, don't tell.

### Law 11: Learn to Keep People Dependent on You
**Application:** Create relationships and dependencies that make you irreplaceable.

### Law 12: Use Selective Honesty and Generosity to Disarm Your Victim
**Application:** Strategic disclosure builds trust. Reveal small vulnerabilities to show authenticity.

### Law 17: Keep Others in Suspended Terror: Cultivate an Air of Unpredictability
**Application:** Controlled unpredictability keeps them off-balance. Don't be a predictable negotiator.

### Law 28: Enter Action with Boldness
**Application:** Hesitation shows weakness. Commit fully to your positions.

### Law 29: Plan All the Way to the End
**Application:** Know every move before you begin. Have exit strategies prepared.

### Law 33: Discover Each Man's Thumbscrew
**Application:** Everyone has leverage points. Find them through deep research.

### Law 42: Strike the Shepherd and the Sheep Will Scatter
**Application:** Identify and neutralize the real decision-maker's objections.

## The Art of War - Situational Applications

### "All warfare is based on deception."
**Negotiation Translation:** Never reveal your true priorities. Make them believe you need X when you actually need Y.

**Tactics:**
- Misdirection on key terms
- Feigned indifference to important elements
- Artificial urgency creation
- Concealed alternatives

### "The supreme art of war is to subdue the enemy without fighting."
**Negotiation Translation:** Best negotiations end before they begin—with positioning that makes resistance futile.

**Tactics:**
- Overwhelming preparation advantage
- Making them want to say yes
- Creating obvious value alignment
- Establishing BATNA superiority

### "If you know the enemy and know yourself, you need not fear the result of a hundred battles."
**Negotiation Translation:** Research their BATNA, psychology, and pressure points as thoroughly as you know your own.

**Tactics:**
- Deep intelligence gathering
- Psychological profiling
- Pressure point mapping
- Continuous learning during negotiation

### "Victorious warriors win first and then go to war."
**Negotiation Translation:** Preparation determines 80% of outcomes. Research everything.

**Tactics:**
- Comprehensive ExecPlan completion
- Scenario planning
- War gaming
- Script preparation
