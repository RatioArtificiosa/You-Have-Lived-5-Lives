# Personality Profiling Quick Reference

## The OCEAN Framework in Negotiation

### Openness to Experience

**High Openness Indicators:**
- Varied career path across industries
- International experience or education
- Startup or innovation background
- Creative hobbies or side projects
- Multiple degrees or diverse education
- Early adopter of technology/trends

**Negotiation Approach for High Openness:**
- Use words: "innovative," "cutting-edge," "pioneering," "disruptive," "visionary"
- Emphasize novelty and unique solutions
- Share creative approaches and out-of-box thinking
- Reference forward-looking trends
- Propose unconventional deal structures
- Appeal to their curiosity

**Avoid:** Rigid processes, appeals to tradition, "this is how we've always done it"

---

**Low Openness Indicators:**
- Long tenure at established companies
- Traditional industry (finance, law, healthcare)
- Conservative dress and presentation
- Standard career progression
- References to established best practices
- Preference for proven methods

**Negotiation Approach for Low Openness:**
- Use words: "proven," "reliable," "established," "time-tested," "industry standard"
- Emphasize track record and precedent
- Provide case studies and testimonials
- Reference respected institutions or experts
- Show detailed implementation plans
- Appeal to security and stability

**Avoid:** Unproven concepts, radical changes, "experimental" approaches

---

### Conscientiousness

**High Conscientiousness Indicators:**
- Detailed, organized communication
- Meeting agendas and structured follow-ups
- Questions about process and timelines
- References to documentation and systems
- Achievement-oriented language
- Punctual and prepared

**Negotiation Approach for High Conscientiousness:**
- Use words: "systematic," "detailed," "process," "guarantee," "measurement"
- Provide comprehensive data and analysis
- Create detailed implementation plans
- Set clear milestones and deadlines
- Document everything in writing
- Show attention to detail

**Avoid:** Vague promises, loose timelines, disorganized presentation

---

**Low Conscientiousness Indicators:**
- Spontaneous communication style
- Flexible or fluid scheduling
- Big-picture focus over details
- Adaptable to changes
- Informal approach
- Creative problem-solving style

**Negotiation Approach for Low Conscientiousness:**
- Use words: "flexible," "simple," "easy," "streamlined," "hassle-free"
- Keep it brief and high-level
- Reduce friction and steps
- Allow for adjustments
- Focus on outcomes, not process
- Make it feel effortless

**Avoid:** Excessive documentation, rigid structures, bureaucratic requirements

---

### Extraversion

**High Extraversion Indicators:**
- Fast-paced, energetic communication
- Frequent use of exclamation points
- Social media activity and networking
- Meeting preference over email
- Talks more than listens initially
- Seeks group consensus

**Negotiation Approach for High Extraversion:**
- Use words: "exciting," "amazing," "opportunity," "win," "celebrate"
- Bring energy and enthusiasm
- Use social proof and group benefits
- Meet in person or video when possible
- Create buzz and excitement
- Appeal to recognition and status

**Avoid:** Long written documents, isolated decision-making, excessive analysis

---

**Low Extraversion Indicators:**
- Measured, thoughtful communication
- Preference for written communication
- One-on-one over group settings
- Listens more than speaks
- Deep focus on specific topics
- Values independence

**Negotiation Approach for Low Extraversion:**
- Use words: "thoughtful," "depth," "quality," "meaningful," "substance"
- Provide written materials in advance
- Allow processing time
- Meet one-on-one
- Respect their space and pace
- Focus on intrinsic value, not social approval

**Avoid:** High-pressure tactics, group meetings, excessive small talk

---

### Agreeableness

**High Agreeableness Indicators:**
- Harmony-focused language
- Concern for others' feelings
- Collaborative approach
- Seeks consensus
- Compromise-oriented
- Relationship-focused

**Negotiation Approach for High Agreeableness:**
- Use words: "partnership," "together," "collaboration," "win-win," "mutual benefit"
- Emphasize shared values and goals
- Show how it benefits the team/organization
- Appeal to fairness and reciprocity
- Build relationship first
- Frame as cooperation, not competition

**Avoid:** Aggressive tactics, win-lose framing, exploitation of niceness

---

**Low Agreeableness Indicators:**
- Direct, blunt communication
- Task-focused over people-focused
- Competitive language
- Challenges ideas readily
- Self-interested framing
- Results-oriented

**Negotiation Approach for Low Agreeableness:**
- Use words: "advantage," "edge," "dominate," "win," "results," "ROI"
- Appeal to self-interest and competitive advantage
- Show how they beat competitors
- Be direct and factual
- Don't waste time on relationship fluff
- Focus on bottom-line impact

**Avoid:** Emotional appeals, excessive rapport-building, vague relationship benefits

---

### Neuroticism (Emotional Stability)

**High Neuroticism Indicators:**
- Anxious or worried language
- Risk-averse statements
- Many questions about what could go wrong
- Seeks reassurance
- Focuses on potential problems
- Needs detailed backup plans

**Negotiation Approach for High Neuroticism:**
- Use words: "secure," "protected," "guaranteed," "safe," "risk-free"
- Address fears directly and thoroughly
- Provide guarantees and safety nets
- Show extensive contingency planning
- Offer risk mitigation strategies
- Provide references and social proof for security

**Avoid:** High-risk propositions, uncertainty, glossing over potential problems

---

**Low Neuroticism Indicators:**
- Confident, calm demeanor
- Risk-tolerant language
- Focus on opportunity over threats
- Decisive and quick to decide
- Comfortable with uncertainty
- Optimistic framing

**Negotiation Approach for Low Neuroticism:**
- Use words: "opportunity," "upside," "potential," "growth," "challenge"
- Focus on gains and benefits
- Emphasize competitive advantages
- Show ambitious possibilities
- Challenge them with stretch goals
- Appeal to achievement and mastery

**Avoid:** Over-emphasizing risks, excessive caution, slow, bureaucratic processes

## MBTI Archetype Negotiation Matrix

### Analysts (NT Types)

**INTJ - The Architect**
- **Trigger Words:** Strategic, logical, optimize, system, efficient, long-term
- **Approach:** Lead with data and strategy. Show the elegant solution. Respect their competence. Get to the point.
- **Avoid:** Emotional manipulation, social proof appeals, lengthy small talk
- **Best Tactics:** Logical framing, efficiency gains, strategic positioning, competence signaling

**INTP - The Logician**
- **Trigger Words:** Analyze, understand, explore, theory, innovative, accurate
- **Approach:** Present the puzzle to solve. Allow exploration. Provide deep technical detail. Respect their expertise.
- **Avoid:** Pressuring for quick decisions, emotional appeals, rigid structures
- **Best Tactics:** Problem-solution framing, accuracy emphasis, intellectual challenge

**ENTJ - The Commander**
- **Trigger Words:** Lead, dominate, results, efficient, challenge, execute
- **Approach:** Match their decisiveness. Show results. Demonstrate competence. Appeal to winning.
- **Avoid:** Indecision, excessive emotion, showing weakness
- **Best Tactics:** Results focus, competitive advantage, executive summary, power positioning

**ENTP - The Debater**
- **Trigger Words:** Innovate, challenge, explore, possibility, debate, disrupt
- **Approach:** Engage in intellectual sparring. Show creative possibilities. Allow brainstorming.
- **Avoid:** Rigid processes, shutting down ideas, being boring
- **Best Tactics:** Novel approaches, creative solutions, strategic debate, option generation

---

### Diplomats (NF Types)

**INFJ - The Advocate**
- **Trigger Words:** Meaning, purpose, authentic, impact, vision, values
- **Approach:** Align with their values. Show authentic purpose. Appeal to making a difference.
- **Avoid:** Pure profit focus, inauthenticity, manipulation
- **Best Tactics:** Values alignment, meaningful impact, authentic connection, vision sharing

**INFP - The Mediator**
- **Trigger Words:** Authentic, values, creative, harmony, ideal, individual
- **Approach:** Respect their individuality. Appeal to ideals. Show personal meaning.
- **Avoid:** Hard sells, pressure, disregard for feelings
- **Best Tactics:** Personal significance, creative freedom, values-based framing, harmony

**ENFJ - The Protagonist**
- **Trigger Words:** Inspire, lead, together, growth, community, develop
- **Approach:** Inspire them. Show team benefits. Appeal to developing others.
- **Avoid:** Isolation, selfish framing, lack of vision
- **Best Tactics:** Team benefits, growth opportunities, inspirational vision, collaborative framing

**ENFP - The Campaigner**
- **Trigger Words:** Exciting, possibility, passion, adventure, inspire, connect
- **Approach:** Bring enthusiasm. Show exciting possibilities. Create connection.
- **Avoid:** Bureaucracy, rigid structure, pessimism
- **Best Tactics:** Excitement building, possibility generation, passion alignment, adventure

---

### Sentinels (SJ Types)

**ISTJ - The Logistician**
- **Trigger Words:** Reliable, proven, responsible, systematic, tradition, duty
- **Approach:** Show track record. Provide systematic plans. Appeal to duty and responsibility.
- **Avoid:** Unproven concepts, disorganization, disregard for rules
- **Best Tactics:** Precedent reference, systematic approach, reliability emphasis, duty appeals

**ISFJ - The Defender**
- **Trigger Words:** Careful, loyal, supportive, stable, service, detail
- **Approach:** Show care and support. Emphasize stability. Appeal to service.
- **Avoid:** Risk, disregard for tradition, aggressive tactics
- **Best Tactics:** Support promises, stability emphasis, detailed care, service orientation

**ESTJ - The Executive**
- **Trigger Words:** Results, efficient, organize, lead, practical, standard
- **Approach:** Show results and efficiency. Be practical and direct. Reference standards.
- **Avoid:** Theory without application, inefficiency, emotional appeals
- **Best Tactics:** Results emphasis, efficiency gains, practical benefits, standard compliance

**ESFJ - The Consul**
- **Trigger Words:** Community, harmony, support, team, care, together
- **Approach:** Show team benefits. Create harmony. Appeal to community.
- **Avoid:** Conflict, selfish framing, disregard for others
- **Best Tactics:** Team benefits, harmony creation, social proof, community alignment

---

### Explorers (SP Types)

**ISTP - The Virtuoso**
- **Trigger Words:** Practical, freedom, hands-on, flexible, craft, immediate
- **Approach:** Let them try it. Emphasize freedom and flexibility. Be practical.
- **Avoid:** Heavy theory, rigid commitments, excessive structure
- **Best Tactics:** Trial offers, flexibility emphasis, hands-on approach, practical benefits

**ISFP - The Adventurer**
- **Trigger Words:** Experience, beauty, individual, flexible, present, authentic
- **Approach:** Appeal to experience. Respect individuality. Be authentic.
- **Avoid:** Pressure, rigid requirements, inauthenticity
- **Best Tactics:** Experience emphasis, individual customization, authentic connection, flexibility

**ESTP - The Entrepreneur**
- **Trigger Words:** Action, now, win, thrill, immediate, results
- **Approach:** Focus on immediate action. Show winning potential. Bring excitement.
- **Avoid:** Long-term planning, excessive analysis, delayed gratification
- **Best Tactics:** Immediate action, winning potential, excitement, quick results

**ESFP - The Entertainer**
- **Trigger Words:** Fun, exciting, now, together, experience, enjoy
- **Approach:** Make it fun and exciting. Focus on the experience. Bring energy.
- **Avoid:** Boring details, isolation, delayed rewards
- **Best Tactics:** Fun framing, exciting possibilities, immediate gratification, social experience

## Rapid Profiling Checklist

### During Initial Contact:

**Communication Analysis:**
- [ ] Do they use bullet points and structure (high Conscientiousness)?
- [ ] Are emails long or short (long = often high Openness/Neuroticism)?
- [ ] How quickly do they respond (fast = high Extraversion)?
- [ ] Do they ask about your weekend (high Agreeableness/Extraversion)?
- [ ] Do they get straight to business (low Agreeableness/Task focus)?

**Meeting Behavior:**
- [ ] Do they prefer video/call or email (Extraversion indicator)?
- [ ] How do they handle silence (uncomfortable = high Extraversion)?
- [ ] Do they take notes extensively (high Conscientiousness)?
- [ ] Are they punctual (high Conscientiousness)?
- [ ] Do they joke around (high Extraversion/Agreeableness)?

**Language Patterns:**
- [ ] "I think/analyze" (Thinkers - T types)
- [ ] "I feel/believe" (Feelers - F types)
- [ ] "Let's explore/possibilities" (Intuitives - N types)
- [ ] "Show me the facts/details" (Sensors - S types)
- [ ] "I need to decide now" (Judgers - J types)
- [ ] "Let's keep options open" (Perceivers - P types)

## Applying the Profiles

### Quick Reference Formula:

1. **Identify Dominant Traits** (2-3 most obvious)
2. **Select Trigger Words** from those trait categories
3. **Choose Appropriate Tactics** from matching archetype
4. **Avoid Known Turn-offs** for those traits
5. **Adapt in Real-time** as you learn more

### Example Application:

**Profile Identified:** High Conscientiousness, Low Agreeableness, INTJ tendencies

**Approach:**
- Lead with detailed data and strategic analysis
- Show efficiency gains and optimization
- Get to the point quickly
- Appeal to competitive advantage
- Avoid emotional appeals and lengthy rapport-building
- Provide written follow-up with all details

**Script Adaptation:**
- Instead of: "I think we could really work well together!"
- Use: "This approach will optimize your operational efficiency by 23% while reducing costs. Here's the data."

---

**Remember:** Personality profiling is a tool, not a cage. People are complex. Use this as a starting hypothesis and refine as you gather more intelligence.
